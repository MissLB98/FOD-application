2.1 Website

You can go to the website HERE

There are three forms of this website. Mobile, desktop and app. I will analyse the desktop version.

Layout and Structure

The desktop version of the website was not designed to be responsive to changing width sizes which kind of hinders you if you want to make the browser window smaller.

The style is very simple and uses dark and light blue accents to differentiate between containers.

Everything looks well organised and divided into categories. The genre and story links are seperated into columns.

The news feed articles and Tweets are displayed with JSON using an API. No images are used to decorate the site that I know of except for your profile picture and story cover art.

Why is this website great?

When you watch a tv-show or read a book, have you ever wished that the characters did anything different or that the plot was different?

Fanfiction.net has an enormous collection of fanfics written by people who want to explore that ‘what if’.

It’s simple, free to use and accessible to nearly anyone.

The community has a good mix of novice and professional writers. I have found stories that are amazing and scarily close to the real thing or even better.

Is there room for improvement?

Unfortunately, yes.

The design is too simple and lacking.

If you compare it to a platform like Wattpad, you can see that more effort could have been put into making it more visually appealing.

Is navigation easy to use?

Fairly easy and understandable as everything is organised into categories upon sub-categories and manageable.

Is information easy to find?

Yes. It has a search input and detailed filter.

What parts of this site are fun and what parts are frustrating?

It’s fun posting your own fanfics, participating in forums and finding that story that is gold amongst the coal.

The frustrating part is that sometimes authors abandon their stories and leave it incomplete.
